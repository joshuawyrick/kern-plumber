H1: Leak Detection in Bakersfield, CA

### SECTION: direct-answer intro
A hidden leak announces itself on your water bill long before it shows on your walls. Electronic leak detection pinpoints hidden and slab leaks in Bakersfield and Kern County homes without exploratory demolition — acoustic listening equipment, pressure testing, and thermal imaging locate the leak first, so the repair opens one spot instead of five.

### SECTION: symptoms
H2: Signs You Have a Hidden Leak
1. A water bill that climbed with no change in household habits
2. The sound of running water when everything is off
3. Warm spots on the floor — the classic sign of a hot-side slab leak
4. Damp carpet, buckled flooring, or a musty smell with no visible source
5. Cracks appearing in flooring or low on the walls
6. The water meter spins with every fixture in the house shut off (an easy self-test — watch the meter for 15 minutes with all water off)

### SECTION: service-details
H2: How Hidden Leaks Get Found
- **Acoustic detection:** sensitive listening equipment picks up the specific sound of pressurized water escaping, even through a concrete slab.
- **Pressure isolation:** the plumbing gets tested in segments to confirm which line is losing water — hot side, cold side, or irrigation.
- **Thermal imaging:** temperature differences reveal wet areas and hot-water leak paths behind walls and under floors.
- **Pinpoint, then repair:** once the leak is marked, you get flat-rate options — a spot repair, a reroute above the slab, or repiping if the pipe itself is at the end of its life.

### SECTION: local-detail
H2: Why Slab Leaks Are a Kern County Specialty
Most Bakersfield-area homes sit on concrete slab foundations with the original copper water lines running underneath — and decades of hard, mineral-heavy water working on that copper from the inside. When a pinhole finally opens under the slab, water can run for weeks unseen. That's the local pattern: high summer water bills hide the early warning, and the leak surfaces as a warm spot on the tile in September.
