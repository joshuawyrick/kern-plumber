H2: When to Call for Gas Line Service
1. The smell of gas (rotten egg odor) anywhere — treat it as an emergency: get out, then call
2. A hissing sound near a gas appliance or line

6. An older home with original gas piping that's never been inspected or pressure-tested

H2: Gas Line Questions
Q: I smell gas — what do I do first?
A: Leave the house immediately — don't flip light switches, don't unplug anything, don't light anything. From outside, call your gas utility's emergency line, and call or text (661) 555-0142 for the repair once the utility has made it safe. Q: Can you run a gas line to my BBQ island or fire pit?
A: Yes — outdoor gas runs are everyday work, sized for the appliance's BTU demand and finished with accessible shut-offs. A permanent line ends the propane-tank shuffle for good.
Q: Do I need a permit for gas line work in Bakersfield?
